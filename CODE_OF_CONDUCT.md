# Code of Conduct

Agent-DID is an open, public, pre-1.0 standardization effort. Productive disagreement is expected. Disrespect is not.

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, code review, documentation review, chat channels created for the project, and external events where someone is representing Agent-DID.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful, precise, and evidence-based.
- Challenge ideas, assumptions, and designs without attacking people.
- Prefer reproducible examples, references, and concrete proposals over vague criticism.
- Welcome contributors with different levels of experience and different technical backgrounds.
- Assume good intent first, then ask clarifying questions before escalating.
- Keep discussions focused on what improves the protocol, the implementations, or the documentation.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, stalking, or sustained hostility.
- Discriminatory or demeaning language related to personal characteristics.
- Personal attacks, insults, or public shaming.
- Doxxing, sharing private information, or reposting private correspondence without permission.
- Deliberately disruptive behavior such as trolling, dogpiling, derailing review threads, or repeated bad-faith argument.
- Publishing private security reports before a coordinated disclosure has been agreed.
- Spam, solicitation, or repetitive self-promotion unrelated to the project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may take any action they consider appropriate to protect the community and the integrity of the project, including:

- requesting a change in behavior
- editing, hiding, locking, or deleting content
- rejecting contributions
- temporarily suspending participation
- permanently banning a participant from project spaces

Enforcement decisions are made at maintainer discretion based on the severity, context, and pattern of behavior.

## Reporting

To report Code of Conduct violations, contact the maintainers at [agent.ai.did@gmail.com](mailto:agent.ai.did@gmail.com) with the subject prefix `[CODE OF CONDUCT]`.

Include as much context as you can:

- where the incident happened
- links or screenshots when available
- the people involved
- why the behavior was harmful or disruptive

Reports will be reviewed as confidentially as practical. The maintainers may need to share limited information with the people involved to investigate and act fairly.

## Project Standard

Agent-DID is trying to mature a public RFC with community input. That only works if people can disagree rigorously without making the space unsafe or exhausting to participate in.

If you participate here, you are expected to uphold that standard.
